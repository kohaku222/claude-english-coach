# ────────────────────────────────────
# TRANSLATE MODE: 外国人は英語を笑わない
# ────────────────────────────────────

Native speakers won't laugh at your English no matter how rough it is — they'll actually try to connect with you, so learning English can be pretty fun.
Regardless of how awkward your English may be, native speakers will never mock you — in fact, they tend to engage with you proactively, which makes language learning a genuinely rewarding experience.
However clumsy your English is, native speakers won't laugh — if anything, they'll go out of their way to chat with you, which is honestly what makes learning a language so much fun.

# ここがポイントです。
# Casual は「try to connect」、Professional は「engage proactively」、Super Native は「go out of their way to chat」と、同じ意味をレベルごとに異なる語彙で表現しています。

However clumsy your English is, native speakers won't laugh — if anything, they'll go out of their way to chat with you.

# TRANSLATE MODE: コーヒーで眠れない
# ────────────────────────────────────

I had coffee, so now I can't sleep at all… I actually want to sleep though.
I made the mistake of having coffee, so now I'm not feeling sleepy at all — which is rather frustrating given that I actually want to get some rest.
I made the mistake of having coffee and now I'm wide awake… of all the times to not be able to sleep.

# ここがポイントです。
# 「コーヒーを飲んでしまった後悔」の表し方として、Casual は単純な because 接続、Super Native は「I made the mistake of ~ing」という構文を使っています。後悔のニュアンスをより強く出したいときは後者が自然です。

I made the mistake of having coffee and now I'm wide awake… of all the times to not be able to sleep.


# ────────────────────────────────────
# 単語・構文: of all the times / wide awake / I made the mistake of ~ing
# ────────────────────────────────────

of all the times to forget your umbrella…
of all the days to run into your ex…

# ────────────────────────────────────
# ポッドキャスト: wishful thinking / be guilty of / a kind way of saying
# ────────────────────────────────────

wishful thinking
I absolutely have hope for peace in the world.
Thinking the project will finish on time without any delays is just wishful thinking.
I'm guilty of checking my phone first thing every morning.
We're all guilty of that.
Saying someone's idea is "interesting" is sometimes just a kind way of saying you don't agree with it.
That's optimistic — which is a kind way of saying it's unlikely.

# ここがポイントです。
# 「That's optimistic」は表面上は褒め言葉ですが、文脈によって「まずないと思う」という婉曲的な否定になります。直接否定を避ける英語の定番テクニックです。

That's optimistic — which is a kind way of saying it's unlikely.


# ────────────────────────────────────
# 単語: skeptic / convinced
# ────────────────────────────────────

skeptic
skeptical
A lot of skeptics said electric cars would never catch on — that's clearly changed.
I'm not convinced this approach will work at scale.
I'm convinced we need to rethink the whole strategy before moving forward.

# ここがポイントです。
# skeptical は「証拠がないから信じない」という論理ベースの態度、cynical は「どうせダメだろう」という感情ベースの否定的態度を指します。

I'm skeptical about the new policy, but I'm willing to give it a chance.


# ────────────────────────────────────
# ポッドキャスト: a lot of times / you might say / Never have I done that
# ────────────────────────────────────

a lot of times
A lot of times, the best solution is the simplest one.
You might say it's risky, but I think it's worth trying.
A lot of times you might say, we talked about this — but is that just wishful thinking?
Never have I done that in my life.
Never have I felt so out of place.

# ここがポイントです。
# 否定副詞（never・rarely・not once）を文頭に置くと主語と助動詞が倒置します。通常形と倒置形の違いを確認してください。

I have never done that in my life.
Never have I done that in my life.

# ────────────────────────────────────
# 単語: burst someone's bubble / brand new / in advance / by the time
# ────────────────────────────────────

burst someone's bubble
I hate to burst your bubble, but the deadline was moved up to next Monday.
Sorry to burst your bubble, but that feature was already built by another team.
brand new
This laptop is brand new — straight out of the box.
That café is brand new to me, even though it's been open for years.
in advance
Please let me know in advance if you can't make it.
We recorded this a bit in advance, so things may have changed by now.
by the time
By the time I got to the station, the train had already left.
By the time you finish reading this, the update will have rolled out.

# ここがポイントです。
# 「by the time」は未来完了形（will have done）と組み合わせることが多く、「その時点ですでに〜している」という時間のズレを表現します。

By the time you hear this, it will have been out for a while.


# ────────────────────────────────────
# 構文: it will have been out a while / transatlantic / charter / last minute / was able to
# ────────────────────────────────────

It will have been out a while by the time you hear this.
Most of you have probably already seen it.
It would do transatlantic flights.
It would charter a plane and then sell any extra tickets really cheap last minute.
I was able to fly to Brussels from Salt Lake City for thirty-five dollars.

# ここがポイントです。
# 「would do / would charter」は過去の繰り返し習慣を表します。状態動詞（exist など）には使えず、その場合は「used to」を使います。

It used to exist, but I don't think it does anymore.
It would charter a plane and sell last-minute tickets at a huge discount.

# ここがポイントです。
# 「could fly」と「was able to fly」の違いに注意してください。過去に実際に達成できた一回限りの成功には「was able to」が正確です。

I was able to fly to Brussels for just thirty-five dollars.

# 相：進行相と完了相の違い（受動態）

He grumbled to his daughter about how he was being treated.
The workers contended that they were being exploited by the company.
He was being treated poorly by the staff.
He had been treated poorly before things changed.

ここがポイントです。
前者は継続している仕打ちのプロセス、後者は区切りのついた過去の経験を表しています。

He was being treated poorly by the staff.

# 受動態の作り方

They treated him badly.
He was treated badly.
He was being treated badly.
He had been treated badly.

ここがポイントです。
受動態は能動態の時制・相をそのままbe動詞に引き継ぎます。単純過去はwas treated、進行相はwas being treated、完了相はhad been treatedになります。

He was being treated badly.

# sinceと基準点の作り方

He entered the nursing home several months ago.
Since then, he had been grumbling to his daughter about how he was being treated.
He grumbled to his daughter about how they had been treating him poorly since he entered the nursing home.

ここがポイントです。
sinceの後ろに来る出来事はそれ自体が基準点になるため、単純過去を使います。もう一つ前の基準点を作る必要はありません。

He grumbled to his daughter about how they had been treating him poorly since he entered the nursing home.

# 相12マス基本形

I cook.
I am cooking.
I have cooked.
I have been cooking for three hours.
I cooked.
I was cooking.
I had cooked.
I had been cooking.
I will cook.
I will be cooking.
I will have cooked.
I will have been cooking.

# 時の副詞節の中は現在形

I had already cooked dinner before he arrived.
I'll have cooked by the time he comes at 7 PM.
I'll have been cooking for hours when he comes.
We will have already left by the time she arrives at the airport.

ここがポイントです。
when, before, by the timeなどの時の副詞節は、未来のことでも現在形を使います。willを重ねないのがルールです。

I'll have cooked by the time he comes at 7 PM.

# 実践例文：料理

I cook breakfast every morning at 6 AM.
I'm cooking in the kitchen right now.

I cooked pasta last night.
I was cooking when the phone rang at 8 PM yesterday.
I'd cooked before he came.
I'd been cooking for hours when he arrived.
I will cook dinner tomorrow.

# 実践例文：勉強・待つ・到着

She studies for three hours every night.
I'm studying in the library.
I've finished studying for the exam.
He had been studying for hours.
She has been waiting for an hour.
I had been waiting for an hour when he came.
The train will arrive at 3 PM tomorrow.

# 確信度の助動詞（Modality）

I'll be studying at this time tomorrow.
I should be studying at this time tomorrow.
I might be studying at this time tomorrow.
I'll probably be studying at this time tomorrow.

ここがポイントです。
willは確信度の高い予測、shouldはやや弱い確信、might/couldは五分五分の推測を表します。willをそのまま使いながらprobablyを足すのも有効です。

I'll probably be studying at this time tomorrow.

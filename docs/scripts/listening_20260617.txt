# get + 過去分詞：状態変化の表現
# ────────────────────────────────────

ここがポイントです。

She is getting tired.

I think she's getting tired now.
I think she's starting to burn out.

# ────────────────────────────────────
# couldn't bring myself to / bring myself to
# ────────────────────────────────────

I couldn't bring myself to ask because he looked so serious.
I couldn't bring myself to tell her the truth.
I finally brought myself to call him back.

ここがポイントです。
couldn't bring myself to は「物理的にできなかった」のではなく「心理的に踏み出せなかった」というニュアンスを持ちます。

I couldn't bring myself to ask.


# ────────────────────────────────────
# 調べる系の動詞：look up / examine / investigate
# ────────────────────────────────────

I looked it up.
I went and looked it up.
I checked the details before replying.
The doctor examined the patient carefully.
The QA team needs to examine the logs carefully before we push the fix to production.
The investigators are looking into the cause of the accident.

ここがポイントです。
look up はサッと調べる、examine は体系的・詳細に調べる、investigate は問題や事件を掘り下げて調査するという強さの違いがあります。

I went and looked it up.


# ────────────────────────────────────
# pass out：2つの意味
# ────────────────────────────────────

He nearly passed out after standing in the heat for three hours.
She passed out from exhaustion after the long flight.
Can you pass out the handouts before the meeting starts?
Can you pass out the sprint review documents before the meeting starts?

ここがポイントです。
pass out には「気を失う」と「配る」の二つの意味があります。文脈で判断することが重要です。

He nearly passed out from exhaustion.

# 赤羽・試験の予定を伝える

Today I'm going to Akabane to take an exam for a job.
I'm heading to Akabane today for a job exam.

# be -ing で未来の予定を表す

I'm going to Akabane today.
I'm going to Akabane right now.

ここがポイントです。
前者は「今日行く予定がある」という確定した未来を表し、後者は「今まさに向かっている最中」という意味になります。時間を示す語（today / right now）が文脈を決定的にします。

I'm going to Akabane today.

# be going to との使い分け

I'm going to Akabane today.
I'm going to go to Akabane today.
I will go to Akabane today.

ここがポイントです。
be -ingは個人的にすでに決めている確定予定、be going toはやや説明的な計画、willはその場で決めた・単純な未来予測に近い響きになります。

I'm going to Akabane today.

# メールに返信する

I need to reply to an email from Marugata.
I've gotta get back to Marugata's email.

# ToDoリストで主語を省略するスタイル

Reply to Marugata's email.
Replying to Marugata's email.

# reply to の前置詞

I need to reply to an email from Marugata.

# end up -ing

I always end up replying to emails late at night.
I ended up staying up way too late last night.
I ended up spending more than I planned.

# right の強調副詞用法

I usually start packing right before my trip, not days in advance.
I don't study every day — it's more like occasional cramming right before exams.
He called me right after the meeting.

# keep it up と stay up の違い

I couldn't keep it up after the first week.
I couldn't stay up that late.

ここがポイントです。
keep it upは「努力・習慣・ペースを維持し続ける」、stay upは「夜更かしする・起きていられる」という意味で、まったく別の表現です。

I tried to exercise every morning, but I couldn't keep it up after the first week.

I wanted to finish the show, but I couldn't stay up that late.

# without exception

Everyone in the class passed the test, without exception.
The rule applies to everyone, without exception.
All flights were delayed, without exception.

# on campus

I really wanna get some sleep, but there's no place to sleep on campus.
There seems to be no place to sleep on campus.
I really need to crash somewhere, but there's nowhere to sleep on campus.

# in campus と on campus の違い

There's no place to sleep on campus.

ここがポイントです。
大学の敷地内を表すときは慣用的に前置詞 on を使います。in ではありません。

There's no place to sleep on campus.

# There seems to be

There seems to be no place to sleep on campus.
There seems to be a problem with the reservation.
There seems to be a misunderstanding between us.

# get over / break up

It took her a few months to get over the breakup.
They decided to break up after dating for two years.
He's still not over his ex.
It's hard to get over something like that.
They broke up out of nowhere.

# ──────────────────────────────────

She's even more beautiful than when we were young.
We went to the same elementary school together.
Ten years after that first crush, we finally had drinks, just the two of us, and it turned out to be a really wonderful night.

# ──────────────────────────────────
# 時制：過去完了と過去の使い分け
# ──────────────────────────────────

I had reached out to her a few days before, and yesterday we finally met up.
After we got back in touch, I went to see her yesterday.

ここがポイントです。
前者は「会った」より前の出来事を過去完了で明示したパターン。後者は after という接続詞で時系列が明らかなため、過去完了なしでも成立します。

I had reached out to her a few days before, and yesterday we finally met up.

# 気遣いの表現：深刻な状況で相手の安否を尋ねる
# ──────────────────────────────────

I've been thinking about you and your brother… how is he?
I don't want to pry, but… I've been thinking about your brother. I hope he's okay.
I've been keeping you both in my thoughts. Please don't feel like you have to update me — but I'm here if you want to talk.

# ──────────────────────────────────
# 気遣いの表現：How's ~ doing との違い
# ──────────────────────────────────

How's your brother doing?
I've been thinking about you and your brother… how is he?

ここがポイントです。
前者は回復を前提としたニュアンスが含まれ、深刻な状況では軽すぎる場合があります。後者は結果を決めつけず、相手に委ねた聞き方です。

I've been thinking about you and your brother… how is he?

# ──────────────────────────────────

And honestly, you don't really need fancy grammar or vocab to just speak English.
I've been realizing lately that no matter how well you can read hard texts, like acing university entrance exams, it doesn't mean you can actually talk.
What's become increasingly clear to me is that fluency in speaking requires an entirely different skill set from academic English proficiency.
Scoring well on entrance exams and actually being able to hold a conversation are simply two different things.
And like… you don't need to be some grammar genius to speak English.
I've really been feeling lately that all that exam-prep stuff — reading dense passages, memorizing obscure vocab — none of that actually makes you a speaker.

# ──────────────────────────────────
# 強い意志・自信の表現
# ──────────────────────────────────

I'm confident I'll be a solid English speaker within a year.
I'm determined to become a fluent English speaker within a year.
I genuinely think I'll be speaking English fluently within a year. I can already feel it happening.

# ──────────────────────────────────
# confident vs convinced の違い
# ──────────────────────────────────

I'm confident I'll be a solid English speaker within a year.
I'm convinced that the new policy will work.

ここがポイントです。
前者は自分自身の内側から来る自信。後者は外部の証拠や状況によって確信させられたニュアンスです。自分の意志・能力への自信には confident を使います。

I'm confident I'll be a solid English speaker within a year.

# 未来完了形・確定した未来の予定
# ────────────────────────────────────────

I will have finished the task by the time you come.

I'm leaving for India in three months and staying for a month.

ここがポイントです。
「in+期間」は今この瞬間が基準点のカウントダウン、「after+期間」は文中の別の出来事が基準点です。

I'm leaving for India in three months.

# ────────────────────────────────────────
# 現在進行形による確定未来 vs will vs be going to
# ────────────────────────────────────────

I'm leaving for India in three months and staying for a month.
I'm going to visit India next year.
I think I'll go to India someday.

ここがポイントです。
be+ingは航空券・予約など手配済みの確定予定、be going toは意図・計画レベル、willはその場の判断・予測です。

I'm leaving for India in three months and staying for a month.

# ────────────────────────────────────────
# 過去の習慣（used to / would）
# ────────────────────────────────────────

I used to play soccer when I was young, but now I can't because of my right knee injury.
I used to play soccer all the time growing up, but my right knee gave out on me so I had to stop.

I used to play badminton all the time growing up, but my left foot injury took me out of it.
I used to play badminton all the time growing up, but a left foot injury forced me to quit.

ここがポイントです。
「used to」は今はしていない過去の習慣の対比を強調します。「would」も過去の習慣を表せますが、「but now」と組み合わせると対比のニュアンスが弱くなります。

I used to play badminton all the time growing up, but a left foot injury forced me to quit.

# ────────────────────────────────────────
# 倒置構文 vs 通常語順
# ────────────────────────────────────────

Never have I committed a minor offense.
I've never committed even the slightest offense.

ここがポイントです。
否定副詞を文頭に置いた倒置構文は文語的・強調的でドラマチックに聞こえます。日常会話では通常語順のほうが自然です。

I've never committed even the slightest offense.

# ────────────────────────────────────────
# during / while / when の使い分け
# ────────────────────────────────────────

We found three critical bugs during the code review.
The model kept hallucinating while I was fine-tuning the parameters.
The server crashed when the load exceeded the threshold.

ここがポイントです。
duringは名詞で期間を範囲指定、whileはS+Vで2つの動作の同時進行、whenはS+Vで時間軸上の点やきっかけを表します。

We found three critical bugs during the code review.

I only study the flashcards which contain new vocabulary.
The script generates a log file, which gets uploaded to S3 automatically.

ここがポイントです。
カンマなしのwhichは先行詞を限定するフィルター、カンマありのwhichは「and it」に近い補足情報の追加です。

The script generates a log file, which gets uploaded to S3 automatically.

# ────────────────────────────────────────
# 所有格 his vs 目的格 him
# ────────────────────────────────────────

I put his gentle leader on so he wouldn't pull too hard.

ここがポイントです。

# ChatGPT + Anki 英語学習法
# ────────────────────────────────────────

My strategy for acquiring English is to use ChatGPT and an app called Anki.
I set up a system prompt for ChatGPT so that it starts acting as my English teacher.

In my daily life, when I want to express my feelings in English but don't know how to say it, I send the Japanese word to the chat, and it sends back the translated version.
Or when I can't understand an English word, I send it over, and ChatGPT also sends back the Japanese meaning along with the IPA.

At the end of the day, I have ChatGPT generate a CSV file containing every word or phrase I used that day.
I then import this file into Anki and turn it into flashcards to study.

My go-to method for picking up English is combining ChatGPT with Anki.
I've got ChatGPT set up with a custom prompt so it acts like a personal English tutor.
Whenever I'm not sure how to phrase something, I just type it in Japanese and get the English version back instantly.
Same goes for words I don't recognize; I throw them at ChatGPT and get the meaning plus IPA right away.
Then at night, I have it compile everything into a CSV, which I dump straight into Anki to turn into flashcards.

# 英語＝体育論（PE比喩）
# ────────────────────────────────────────

I would just say that English is more like PE than math, in the sense that it needs daily practice.
What I mean is, math or physics can sometimes improve all at once, but PE doesn't work that way.
Your muscles need time to repair, and that process takes two or three days.
This shows that you can't get stronger overnight.

What I'm trying to say is that English is more like a sport than a subject like math.
It demands daily reps, not occasional cramming.
With math or physics, things can click all at once.
But with something physical, your muscles literally need two or three days to repair and rebuild.
You just can't rush that kind of strength.
